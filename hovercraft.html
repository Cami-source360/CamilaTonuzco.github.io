Project 06

Hovercraft

Design, Fabrication, and Performance Evaluation of a Compact Amphibious Vehicle

Project Details

  • Completed: November 2024
  • Core Focus: Multidisciplinary System Design, Fluid Dynamics, Green Energy Integration, Rapid Prototyping

Introduction & Engineering Challenge

This project was developed as part of a multidisciplinary engineering challenge to design, manufacture, and validate a high-efficiency hovercraft capable of transitioning seamlessly between smooth solid terrain and water. A primary design constraint was the successful integration of an onboard renewable energy generation mechanism to supplement power consumption.

The development process began with a comprehensive literature review focused on identifying the critical variables governing aerodynamic lift and energy harvesting systems.

Air-Cushion Geometry

While a circular profile maximizes pure vertical lift efficiency, a hybrid rectangular geometry with rounded edges and an aerodynamic arc-shaped bow was selected. This configuration significantly reduced the drag coefficient (Cd) and minimized turbulent airflow, improving forward velocity and overall stability.

Energy Generation

To determine the optimal energy harvesting strategy, weighted decision matrices were used to evaluate solar panels, micro wind turbines, and piezoelectric systems. Solar photovoltaic technology was selected due to its superior power-to-weight ratio and ease of structural integration.

Design and Prototyping

The vehicle was developed through an iterative concurrent-engineering workflow in which 3D CAD modeling directly guided the physical manufacturing process. By maintaining the digital designs ahead of fabrication, the team established a rapid prototyping pipeline that streamlined subsystem refinement and testing.

Air-Cushion System

As the most technically demanding subsystem, the lift mechanism required multiple prototype iterations. Dimensional optimization and material testing were conducted to achieve uniform air distribution and eliminate ground friction.

Propulsion System

The propulsion subsystem was engineered to deliver reliable thrust performance across both land and water environments. Fans and propellers were selected through a combination of simulation-based analysis and empirical static-thrust testing.

Energy Integration

Photovoltaic panels were strategically integrated into the upper chassis geometry to maximize solar radiation capture. The electrical circuitry was designed to interface directly with the onboard battery system for continuous energy supplementation.

Directional Control

Precision maneuverability was achieved through the implementation of a responsive steering system combined with aerodynamic rudders optimized for low-speed control authority.

Ingress Protection

To ensure electronic reliability during aquatic operation, custom watertight enclosures and sealing features were designed and fabricated to protect sensitive components from water ingress.

Manufacturing & Assembly

The physical structure utilized a hybrid manufacturing approach combining laser-cut MDF for the primary chassis with 3D-printed components for complex internal geometries. The architecture employed a modular fastener system using screws and bolts, enabling rapid disassembly, efficient troubleshooting, and seamless subsystem upgrades.

All electrical systems were implemented following structured schematic designs to ensure reliability, maintainability, and safe power distribution.

Competition Performance

The finalized hovercraft was evaluated in a rigorous university-level engineering competition consisting of three independent performance categories:

  • Ground Agility Test: Achieved Second Place through high maneuverability and low surface friction.
  • Water Agility Test: Secured First Place by demonstrating excellent hydrodynamic stability and precise directional control.
  • Water Race: Earned First Place due to optimized propulsion efficiency and rapid rudder response.

Key Milestone: By securing top placements in two of the three competition events, the team achieved the title of Overall Competition Champions, validating the effectiveness of the engineering design, manufacturing methodology, and system integration strategy.

Project 01

Pinion Double-Rack System

Modeling, instrumentation, and hybrid control of a mechanical-electrical system

Presented at the 7th IEEE Colombian Conference on Automatic Control (CCAC 2025) as part of the Reliability, Industrial Systems and Education in Control Technical Session.
Presented at the XIV Congreso Internacional de Ingenieria Mecatronica y Automatizacion (CIIMA 2025) as part of the Control Technical Session.

Project Details

CIIMA 2025 (Published) - IEEE CCAC 2025 (Published)

Pinion System Setup

This project focuses on the design, construction, and analytical modeling of a double-rack mechanism for advanced system identification and hybrid dynamics research.

The initial phase of the project was presented at the XIV Congreso Internacional de Ingenieria Mecatronica y Automatizacion (CIIMA 2025). In that study, we implemented a physical prototype consisting of a DC motor, dual opposing racks, and elastic springs, instrumented with current, displacement, and angular velocity sensors.

Project Render

The research aimed to experimentally identify uncertain dynamic parameters such as armature resistance, inductance, and viscous friction of the DC motor. Data was gathered from the physical system using an Arduino-controlled acquisition setup and analyzed using MATLAB/Simulink.

The resulting dynamic model was refined through grey-box and black-box identification methods, demonstrating strong agreement between simulation and experimental measurements.

Building on this foundation, an extension of the work was presented at the 7th IEEE Colombian Conference on Automatic Control (CCAC 2025). This second publication introduced a formal Hybrid Dynamical System (HDS) perspective, modeling the switching behavior inherent to the pinion double-rack mechanism.

The system behavior was represented using hybrid automata capable of describing transitions between discrete motion phases based on spring deformation and gear angular displacement.

The hybrid model employed a state-space formulation incorporating the previously identified parameters. Transitions between states were triggered by guard conditions linked to spring deformation limits. Each operating mode corresponded to either upward or downward motion of one of the racks.

The simulation framework was implemented in MATLAB/Simulink with switching logic, enabling evaluation of the system under realistic constraints including friction effects and power consumption.

Energy Consumption Analysis

A key contribution of this work was the analysis of energy consumption under hybrid control conditions.

By integrating the absolute current over time, the simulation results were compared against experimental measurements, revealing a 30.8% increase in real energy consumption caused by electrical and mechanical inefficiencies not fully captured in the theoretical model.

Additionally, the system behavior in phase space validated the effectiveness of the hybrid representation in accurately predicting switching dynamics and state transitions.
